Architectural Highlights

Akshardham Temple is a modern architectural marvel that draws inspiration from centuries-old Vedic traditions. Constructed using pink sandstone and white marble, its design reflects the beauty and intricacy of traditional Indian temple architecture.

Materials Used

Pink Sandstone

The temple's exterior is crafted from pink sandstone, symbolizing warmth and spirituality. This material was sourced from Rajasthan and intricately carved by skilled artisans.

White Marble

The temple's sanctum and key interiors are made of white marble, reflecting purity and peace. This marble was sourced from the quarries of Makrana, the same marble used in the Taj Mahal.

Intricate Carvings

The temple is adorned with thousands of carvings depicting Hindu deities, saints, and stories from scriptures. The central dome is a testament to architectural brilliance, symbolizing the cosmos and divine energy.

Engineering Feats

Despite being built in modern times, no steel or iron was used in the construction, staying true to ancient architectural practices. The structure is designed to withstand earthquakes and other natural calamities.
